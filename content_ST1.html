Renewable Energy Solutions

How transitioning to clean energy sources can combat climate change and create a sustainable future for all.

☀️ Solar Energy

Solar energy harnesses the power of sunlight to generate electricity through photovoltaic (PV) panels. It is currently the fastest-growing energy source globally, with installed capacity exceeding 1,000 gigawatts worldwide. The cost of solar panels has fallen by over 89% since 2010, making it the cheapest form of new electricity generation in many countries.

Residential solar installations allow homeowners to generate their own electricity, reduce energy bills, and even sell surplus energy back to the grid. Solar farms, ranging from small community projects to vast desert installations, now power millions of homes and businesses. In the UK, solar energy has grown from virtually nothing to contributing over 4% of the national electricity supply.

How Solar Panels Work

Solar PV panels contain cells made of semiconducting material, usually silicon. When photons from sunlight strike these cells, they knock electrons loose, creating a flow of electricity. This direct current (DC) is then converted to alternating current (AC) by an inverter for use in homes and businesses. Modern panels can achieve efficiency rates of 20-25%, with research pushing towards 40% using advanced materials.

💨 Wind Power

Wind energy uses the kinetic energy of moving air to spin turbine blades, which drive generators to produce electricity. Modern wind turbines can reach heights of over 200 metres, with blades spanning more than 100 metres. A single large turbine can generate enough electricity to power over 1,500 homes.

Offshore wind farms, located in seas and oceans, benefit from stronger and more consistent winds. The UK is the world leader in offshore wind, with dozens of operational wind farms across the North Sea, Irish Sea, and English Channel. The industry supports over 30,000 jobs and continues to grow rapidly as costs decline and technology improves.

Onshore vs Offshore Wind

Onshore wind farms are less expensive to build and maintain but face challenges related to land use, visual impact, and noise. Offshore farms, while costlier, generate more electricity per turbine due to higher and more consistent wind speeds. Both forms play essential roles in a balanced, diverse renewable energy mix.

💧 Hydroelectric Energy

Hydroelectric power generates electricity by channelling the force of flowing or falling water through turbines. It is one of the oldest and most established forms of renewable energy, providing approximately 16% of global electricity. Large-scale dams, such as the Three Gorges Dam in China, can generate enormous amounts of power — enough to supply entire cities.

Small-scale hydroelectric installations, often called micro-hydro, can provide clean energy to rural and remote communities without the environmental impact of large dams. Run-of-river systems, which do not require dams, are a lower-impact alternative that can generate reliable power while preserving natural river ecosystems.

✅ Benefits of Renewable Energy

Transitioning to renewable energy sources offers numerous benefits that extend well beyond reducing greenhouse gas emissions:

  • Climate protection: Renewables produce little to no direct carbon dioxide emissions during operation, directly addressing the root cause of climate change.
  • Air quality improvement: Unlike fossil fuels, clean energy sources do not produce harmful pollutants such as particulate matter, nitrogen oxides, or sulphur dioxide.
  • Job creation: The renewable energy sector is a major employer, with new opportunities in manufacturing, installation, maintenance, research, and policy.
  • Energy independence: Countries that invest in domestic renewable energy reduce their reliance on imported fossil fuels, improving national security.
  • Cost savings: Once installed, solar and wind systems have minimal operating costs, providing affordable, long-term energy.

🎓 What Students Can Do

As students, there are many practical steps you can take to support the renewable energy transition and promote climate action:

  • Advocate for campus sustainability: Push for solar panels on university buildings, improved energy efficiency, and divestment from fossil fuels.
  • Choose green energy providers: When living independently, opt for electricity tariffs that use 100% renewable sources.
  • Reduce energy waste: Turn off lights, unplug devices, and use energy-efficient appliances to lower your personal demand.
  • Learn and share: Take courses on sustainability, attend lectures, and share knowledge with peers to build awareness.
  • Support innovation: Engage with student research projects, innovation competitions, and sustainability societies.
